This table shows a history of recent updates to the Microsoft Writing Style Guide.

Contact msstyle@microsoft.com for archives of earlier history.

Slashes		Added the guideline that when words are joined with a slash, the word after the slash is capitalized if the word before the slash is. Updated examples.

August 9, 2017NumbersClarified that in measurements where the unit is spelled out, the plural form is used unless the number is 1, in which case the singular form of the unit of measure is used.

May 24, 2017Writing for all abilitiesAdded the guideline that words like and, plus, and about should be spelled out because screen readers can misread text that uses the plus sign (+) or tilde (~).
May 24, 2017plus sign (+)Updated with information about using the plus sign (+) as a replacement for words like and, over, and plus. Updated examples.
May 22, 2017Quotation marksAdded a guideline that, in most content, use double quotation marks (" ") not single quotation marks (' ').

May 11, 2017Use certain words carefullyDeleted this subtopic in the Word choice topic. The guidelines that were here can be found in the Security, safety, and privacy content topic (for the words safe, private, secure, and protect) and in the please, sorry, and thank you topics.

September 9, 2016Cloud-computing term listAdded guidance for conversations as a platform (CaaP).
